<p><p>LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging) in the automotive market comprises three main types: </p><p>1. **Mechanical Rotating LiDAR**: This type uses rotating components to emit laser beams, providing a 360-degree view of the surroundings, ideal for high-resolution mapping.</p><p>2. **Hybrid Solid-State LiDAR**: Combining rotating elements with solid-state technology, this type offers improved reliability and lower costs while maintaining decent range and resolution.</p><p>3. **Solid-State LiDAR**: This technology uses fixed laser arrays without moving parts, resulting in compact designs, enhanced durability, and reduced manufacturing costs, making it suitable for various automotive applications.</p></p>
