<p><p>Clinical trial drug sourcing involves procuring medications needed for clinical studies, with two primary market types: direct sourcing and open market sourcing. Direct sourcing refers to obtaining drugs directly from manufacturers or sponsors, ensuring high-quality products and often streamlined processes. In contrast, open market sourcing involves acquiring drugs from various vendors or distributors, providing flexibility in terms of availability and pricing. Both methods play crucial roles in supporting the successful execution of clinical trials while adhering to regulatory standards.</p></p>
