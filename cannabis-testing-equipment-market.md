<p><p>The cannabis testing equipment market comprises chromatography-based and spectroscopy-based analyzers. Chromatography-based analyzers, such as High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) and Gas Chromatography (GC), separate compounds in cannabis samples for accurate potency and purity assessment. Spectroscopy-based analyzers, including Near-Infrared (NIR) and Ultraviolet-Visible (UV-Vis) spectroscopy, measure light absorption to identify and quantify cannabinoids and terpenes. Both methods play a critical role in ensuring product safety, quality control, and regulatory compliance in the rapidly growing cannabis industry.</p></p>
